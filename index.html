🎞️ MMT Scanner

Developer: Martin  |  Support: mmtelecine@gmail.com  |  Price: €5,850

AspectProsConsWorkflow Impact
QualityBest scanner. Up to 5.5K. Full colour/exposure control.Slowest (6–15 fps, optimal 10–12 fps)Essential for premium output — reduces heavy post-production corrections
ReliabilityExcellent output filesProjector-style transport (splice failure risk). Unreliable supplier.Films MUST be cleaned/lubricated. Experienced technicians required.

🛠️ 1. Preparation & Inspection

  • Cleaning: Film must be cleaned and lubricated before threading (MMT standard)
  • Splice check: Manually verify the stability of the leading splice (leader tape)
  • Reel setup: Full supply reel on the left spindle, empty take-up reel on the right spindle
  • Orientation: Perforation faces towards you when mounting on the left arm

🧵 2. Threading Process

  • Unroll approx. 0.5m of leader from the left supply reel
  • Guide the film precisely around the left tension rollers — film must sit perfectly centred
  • Open the gate → thread the leader → align perforations exactly with the sprockets
  • Film must lie absolutely flat in the guide rails — no arching or bowing
  • ⚠️ Only close the gate if no mechanical resistance is felt
  • Secure at left and right sprockets — leave a slight loop (slack) on both sides
  • Guide through the right tension rollers and insert securely into the take-up core
  • Rotate the right reel by hand 2–3 full turns until film is taut

💻 3. Software Setup

  • Check configuration. If wrong: File > Load Configuration → select the most recent file
  • Film Icon (top right) → enter Order Number & Media Number → Save
  • Verify Focus, Camera Resolution and correct Film Format (e.g. Super 8 / Regular 8) on the monitor

🚀 4. Starting the Scan

  • Software: Click the RED BUTTON (top left) to start recording
  • Machine: Immediately after, move the lever downwards to the red REC position
  • Use the Jog function to move the film a few frames forward → verify stable transport
  • Only start once the film runs smoothly and centred through the gate

⚠️ 5. Safety Checklist

Clicking sound: clicking = misaligned perforations → PRESS STOP IMMEDIATELY!
No software changes without prior consultation. Report accidental clicks immediately!
  • After threading: briefly check the digital image for hair or dust in the gate
  • Film loose or looping? → re-check seating on the tension rollers

🛠️ I. Hardware Setup

  • [1] Press the large red button on the front → power on
  • [2] Attach the full supply reel to the left arm → screw the black nut onto the spindle. HAND-TIGHT ONLY! Do NOT over-tighten!
  • [3] Film path: lead film OVER first large guide roller → UNDER the black dancer roller → into the Film Gate → after gate UNDER next small roller → OVER final large guide roller on the right
  • ⚠️ ORIENTATION: Perforation must face strictly TOWARDS THE MACHINE (backwards/inwards)
  • [4] Align the Lightpin (red laser at the gate) EXACTLY onto the perforation — required for software to recognise individual frames
  • [5] Secure the film to the empty right take-up reel

💻 II. Software Configuration

  • Create a new album → Album Title: Order Number (e.g. GB-10073) → Export Drive: always Drive D: → OK
  • Click the correct Order Number in the list
  • Click PREVIEW → let film run briefly → check first frames → correct alignment if needed
  • Rewind film back to leader → close the preview window

🚀 III. Starting the Scan

  • Step 1 (Software): Click the START button (bottom centre)
  • Step 2 (Machine): Flip the switch to FORWARD
⚠️ Only set the machine to FORWARD AFTER clicking Start in the software!

📤 Export Guide

Exports ONLY at end of shift (Overnight)! Running scan + export simultaneously is STRICTLY PROHIBITED — leads to dropped frames!
  • Navigate to the "View" tab → click "Export" at the bottom
  • Click the Order Number (highlighted in blue) → all clips are automatically selected
  • Verify settings: Resolution typically 1080p in 4:3, export speed usually 18fps (Super 8 / Regular 8)
  • Right-click on a setting value → apply to all selected files
  • Click "Export" at the bottom

🎬 Super 8 Film

Introduced by Kodak in 1965. Larger image area than Regular 8, simple cartridge-loading system.

🔑 Key Identifier

Small square perforations alongside a large image area. The frame takes up most of the film width.

📦 Loading System

Plastic cartridge system — no manual threading of camera required. Easy-load design from 1965.

CategoryDetails
Film width8 mm (Super 8 gauge)
Resolutionapprox. 1.5K–2K
Aspect ratio1.33:1 (standard)
UsageHome movies, student films, experimental projects
ScannersFilm-Digital (sound), MMT (silent), RetroScan (silent)
Sound optionsSilent or magnetic/optical soundtrack

🎨 Silent vs. Sound Film

  • Silent Super 8: No soundtrack. Slightly larger image area → better picture quality.
  • Sound Super 8: Magnetic or optical soundtrack along the edge. Slightly smaller image area.
💡 Always check if the film has sound! ONLY the Film-Digital machine can digitize the soundtrack. FD2 for Super 8 magnetic sound.

📦 Storage & Handling

  • Cool, dry, dark. Ideal: 10–15°C, 40–50% humidity
  • Handle by cartridge edges or spool only — do not bend
  • Inspect for dust, scratches and damage before scanning

🎬 Regular 8 / Standard 8

Introduced in the 1930s as a compact, affordable alternative to 16mm. Vintage look, popular with collectors and experimental filmmakers.

🔑 Key Identifier

Large perforations that are similar in size to the frame itself. The image area is noticeably smaller than Super 8.

🔇 Sound?

Almost always silent. Sound versions are very rare. Always inspect the edge for a brown magnetic stripe before assuming silent.

CategoryDetails
Film width8 mm (Regular/Standard 8)
Resolutionapprox. 1K–1.5K
UsageHome movies, educational films, archival, experimental
ScannersFilm-Digital (sound), MMT (silent), RetroScan (silent)
SoundMostly silent; rare magnetic/optical soundtracks
💡 Almost all Regular 8 films are silent. Still always check! FD1 (Coming Soon) for Regular 8 magnetic sound.

🎬 9.5mm Pathé Film

Introduced in 1922 by Pathé Frères as "Pathé Baby". First popular home cinema format in Europe. Unique central perforation between each frame.

⚠️ Handle with extreme care!

The single central perforation is delicate and prone to tearing. Always use cotton gloves, rewind slowly, and never apply strong tension. Misalignment during scanning will immediately tear the film.

CategoryDetails
Film width9.5 mm
Unique featureSingle central perforation between each frame
Resolutionapprox. 1.5K–2K
ScannersMMT Film Scanner, RetroScan
SoundRare optical sound (later versions from 1930s onward)
Base materialOriginally cellulose nitrate, later triacetate/polyester
💡 Clean gently before scanning — dust is very visible on this small format. Use a soft brush only.

🎬 16mm Film

Introduced by Eastman Kodak in 1923. Cost-effective alternative to 35mm. Still widely used for documentaries, student films and art projects.

🔑 Key Identifier

One perforation per frame on one side only. Significantly wider strip than 8mm formats — clearly visible difference.

🔊 AEO-Light Note

Optical sound on 16mm sits 26 frames ahead of the picture. Enter this offset in AEO-Light under "Sound Timecode Advanced".

Resolutionapprox. 2K–3K
UsageDocumentary, educational, indie, art projects
ScannersFilm-Digital (sound), MMT (silent), RetroScan (silent)
Sound optionsSilent, Optical, Magnetic

🎬 35mm Film

The gold standard of professional filmmaking. Introduced by Thomas Edison & William Dickson in the late 19th century. Still used by Nolan, Tarantino, and PTA today.

🔥 Always check for Nitrate!

Look for the word "NITRATE" printed on the film edge before doing anything else. Absence of the marking does NOT guarantee safety. If in doubt — inform leadership immediately.

CategoryDetails

Perforations4 per frame, both sides
Resolutionapprox. 4K–6K
UsageProfessional cinema productions, archival, high-end art
ScannerRetroScan ONLY
Aspect ratios1.85:1 or 2.39:1 (Cinemascope)
SoundOptical or magnetic track along the strip
💡 Fun fact: Christopher Nolan, Quentin Tarantino and Paul Thomas Anderson still shoot on 35mm — they swear by its authentic cinematic look.

⚠️ Vinegar Syndrome

Chemical deterioration of cellulose acetate film. Releases acetic acid — self-accelerating decay process. Highly contagious within archival collections.

🚩 Symptoms

  • Odour: Sharp, distinctive vinegar smell (intensifies as decay progresses)
  • Deformation: Shrinkage, curling, warping (buckling)
  • Texture: Increased brittleness — film becomes fragile and prone to tearing
  • Visual: White crystalline deposits or "Channeling" (bubbles/voids between base and emulsion)

🛡️ PPE — Mandatory

  • Nitrile or vinyl gloves at all times
  • Mask with organic vapour/multi-gas cartridges if odour is strong
  • Safety goggles when handling multiple degraded reels
  • Ventilation: mandatory — work only in well-ventilated areas or under exhaust hoods
  • Open cans away from your face (initial burst of concentrated vapours!)

🖥️ Scan Protocol (Critical)

Machine: Use the RetroScan Universal Mark II 4K ONLY
Speed: Enable "Scan Slow" for the gentlest possible transport
Aperture: At least F5.6 (deep enough depth of field for warped film)
Recommendation: Scan in 2K (shorter lens → more light at F5.6)
After scan: Clean the ENTIRE machine EXTREMELY THOROUGHLY
Stop rule: Film keeps snapping? STOP IMMEDIATELY — the film is beyond saving

📦 Handling & Storage

  • Keep affected reels strictly separated from healthy film
  • No solvents without expert advice
  • Use A-D Strips (Acid-Detector) to measure acidity level
  • Label containers clearly as "Vinegar Syndrome"
  • Storage: below 18°C, 30–50% relative humidity. Advanced decay: cold/frozen storage required.

⚠️ Tenite Reel Decay

Chemical breakdown of the plastic spool, commonly found on grey 50ft 8mm reels. Frequently mistaken for mould — requires different handling.

🔑 vs. Mould — Key Difference

Tenite: Crystalline/chalky, stays on the spool, does not spread.
Mould: Fuzzy/fibrous, organic, can spread to film.

✅ Good News

The film material itself is usually unaffected. Clean the spool, then scan normally.

🚩 Symptoms

  • Appearance: Powdery, waxy or chalky white residue on the spool rim
  • Odour: Vinegar or rancid-butter smell (acetic/butyric acid off-gassing)

🛡️ PPE

  • Nitrile or vinyl gloves
  • P2/FFP2 dust mask sufficient. Use organic vapour filters if odour causes discomfort.
  • Work in well-ventilated areas only

🧼 Cleaning & Scanning

  • Dry cleaning: gently remove residue with a soft, dry brush or cloth
  • NO strong solvents! They accelerate decay and create additional fumes
  • Once the spool is cleaned → scan the film as normal

🦠 Mouldy Film — Biological Hazard

Mould is a biological growth that thrives on moisture and organic material (like film emulsion). Health hazard! Produces spores and mycotoxins that cause respiratory issues and skin irritation.

🚫 STOP — NO MACHINE PASSAGE!

Contaminated films must NOT be run through any machine. Spores contaminate the transport path, rollers and optics — and can transfer to every subsequent customer's film.

🔍 Identification

  • Appearance: Fuzzy, patchy, or thread-like (web-like) growths on film surface or edges
  • Colours: Commonly white, grey, green, or black spots
  • Odour: Distinctive musty/earthy smell when opening canisters
  • ⚠️ Do not confuse with Tenite "bloom" — mould is organic/fibrous; Tenite is chalky and stays on the plastic spool

🛡️ PPE — ALL MANDATORY

  • Respiratory: FFP2 or FFP3 mask — mandatory!
  • Gloves: Nitrile or vinyl
  • Eyes: Goggles or safety glasses
  • Clothing: Disposable lab coat or coveralls
  • Isolation: Keep strictly separate. Label all containers "MOULD / BIOHAZARD"

🧼 Cleaning & Decontamination

  • Work in well-ventilated areas or under fume hood only
  • Dry cleaning: gently remove surface mould with a soft brush — do not brush vigorously!
  • HEPA vacuum with micro-attachments to safely capture loose spores
  • NO liquids/moisture — mould thrives on moisture
  • Dispose of used swabs and cloths in sealed bags as biohazardous waste

💧 Water Damage

Water damage is a severe threat to film integrity. Moisture causes the gelatin emulsion to swell and become tacky. Leads to permanent image loss, physical distortion, and is the primary catalyst for mould growth.

⚠️ EXTREMELY SENSITIVE!
Never forcefully unroll damp or sticky reels. Do NOT rub or apply pressure to the surface — the image can be wiped away instantly.

🔍 Visual Identification

  • Tide lines, marble-like patterns where the emulsion has shifted or reacted
  • Emulsion layer becomes soft and tacky when wet
  • Advanced: emulsion may flake off, "ferrotype" (stick to next layer), or rub off with slightest touch

💨 Drying & Restoration

  • Air-dry in a cool, well-ventilated environment
  • If not too sticky: gently unspool sections onto a clean, non-abrasive surface
  • NO heat — no hair dryers or heaters (causes emulsion cracks and base warping)

🖥️ Scanning Notes

  • Manually inspect every reel for sticky spots before mounting
  • Film layers fused together (blocking)? → DO NOT place on scanner
  • Scan with minimal tension — dried emulsion may be brittle
  • Use only soft brushes or lint-free cloths for light residue

🔥 Nitrate Film (Hazardous Material)

Cellulose Nitrate was the industry standard until the mid-20th century. Inherently unstable and highly flammable. Contains its own oxygen source — once ignited, almost impossible to extinguish. Almost exclusively found in 35mm.

🔍 Identification

  • Brownish/amber tint (especially at edges), severe buckling, white powdery crystals
  • Distinctive sharp, acrid or "chemical" smell → close can immediately and proceed with caution
  • Look for the word "NITRATE" printed on the film edge — absence of marking does NOT guarantee safety!
🔥 Critical Fire Safety:
• Auto-ignition possible at 38°C (100°F) or through friction
• Burning nitrate produces lethal gases (nitrogen oxides)
• Avoid high-speed unspooling or friction-heavy transport

🛡️ Handling & Storage

  • Gloves mandatory. Wear a mask if film is powdery or off-gassing
  • Cold storage required — ideally below 5°C
  • Store only in designated nitrate cabinets or well-ventilated areas

🖥️ Scanning

  • NEVER scan Stage 2 (sticky) or Stage 3 (powdery) nitrate
  • Scanner light source must be LED (cold light) — no heat build-up in the gate
  • Emergency plan: Know the location of the nearest fire exit and specialised fire extinguisher BEFORE starting any nitrate scan
  • Always inform leadership before scanning nitrate

🔊 Magnetic Sound (Magstripe)

Think of it as a long piece of cassette tape glued directly onto the film edge. The film carries audio right alongside the images.

Mono

One brown stripe on one side only.

Duo / Stereo

Thick stripe + thin "balance stripe" on the opposite edge.

Full Coat

Entire strip is brown. Audio-only, no image (Sepmag).

⚙️ Which machine?

  • FD2 → Super 8 magnetic sound ✅
  • FD1 → Regular 8 magnetic sound (Coming Soon)
  • MMT and RetroScan cannot capture magnetic sound
⚠️ Magnetic fields erase the sound — keep away from strong magnets and speakers!
"Shedding" (brown dust in scanner) → STOP IMMEDIATELY. Clean the sound head on FD1/FD2 regularly.

〰️ Optical Sound

Optical sound is like a "barcode for audio". Unlike magnetic sound, it is photographed directly onto the film. The audio is literally part of the image itself.

Variable Area

Looks like a zigzag mountain range. Most common modern type. The width of the waveform encodes the audio.

Variable Density

Looks like a ladder with fine horizontal bars. Older technology. The density of lines encodes the audio.

⚙️ 2-Step Workflow

  • Step 1 — Capture (RetroScan): Scan the film. The soundtrack must be fully visible in the frame.
  • Step 2 — Extract (AEO-Light): AEO-Light reads the waveform and converts it to a WAV audio file. Can digitally ignore many physical scratches.
⚠️ Dust and scratches on the optical track cause pops, clicks or hiss — keep the gate 100% clean!
16mm optical sound sits 26 frames ahead of the picture → enter this offset in AEO-Light.

🎙️ Sepmag (Separate Magnetic)

Picture and sound stored on two completely separate reels. No soundtrack on the film strip itself.

⚠️ Critical Notes for Lab Technicians

  • Dual Capture: Sepmag requires two separate processes — scan the film for picture, then play the Full Coat reel through a sound head for audio
  • Sync: Files must be manually synced in post (Premiere Pro or DaVinci Resolve) using the clapper board or start marks at the head of each reel
  • Handling: Treat the Full Coat reel like any magnetic film — keep away from magnets, check for shedding (brown dust) before playback

💻 AEO-Light: Extracting Optical Sound

1. Start a Project

Open AEO-Light → File > New Project → select your source folder. Note: DPX or TIFF image sequences preferred, but video files are also supported.

2. Mark the Soundtrack ⚠️ Most Critical Step!

  • Once the film frame is visible: set the Bounding Box — drag vertical lines left and right to exactly enclose the optical soundtrack
  • CRITICAL: Do NOT drag lines into the perforations (sprocket holes) or the image area → result: loud noise, distortion, or hum!

3. Set Frame Pitch

  • Drag horizontal lines to the dividing line between two film frames (top and bottom)
  • Check the visualisation window: red and green waveforms should blend smoothly into a yellow pattern → sound is perfectly in sync

4. Configure Audio Settings

  • Track type: select Mono or Stereo
  • Sync offset: For 16mm film, the sound is typically 26 frames ahead of the picture. Enter this value under "Sound Timecode Advanced" for perfect lip sync

5. Test & Calibrate

  • New Sample: generates a 5-second audio preview
  • Calibration tip: If the volume level fluctuates → click "Calibrate". This compensates for uneven lighting during the scan → much cleaner audio

6. Export

  • "Extract Audio" tab → choose filename → click Extract
  • Software outputs a finished WAV audio file
  • Optionally: also exportable as video with embedded audio

✅ Quick-Check: Scan Process (SOP)

1. Pre-Production

  • App Sync: Does the Order ID and total reel count match the physical job?
  • Film Inspection: Checked format (8mm / 9.5mm / 16mm / 35mm) & quality?
  • Document damages/anomalies immediately in the Warehouse App!
  • Cleanliness: Machine, film path and gate completely free of dust?
  • Software Setup: MMT/FD — Order number correctly entered? RetroScan — New album created with correct Order number?

2. Machine Setup

  • Threading: Perforation correctly oriented for the scanner being used? Film path correct?
  • Technical: Focus sharp? Histogram (Exposure) centred?
  • Audio: Optical track visible or magnetic head (FD1/FD2) aligned?

3. Active Scan

  • Leader: Captured at least 3 seconds of leader?
  • Monitoring: Active supervision of stability, focus and sound

4. Completion & QA

  • File Check: Quick visual inspection (Beginning / Middle / End)
  • App Status: Set status to "Scanned" in the Warehouse App?
  • Packaging: Film safely returned to its original, labelled container?
  • Logistics: Return the complete order (box/film) to the CORRECT Bay!
💡 In case of critical damage (e.g. Vinegar Syndrome): always consult leadership BEFORE scanning!

Keep your workspace clean and tidy

Keep your workstation clean and leave it in an orderly condition for the next person.

4
Switch off all machines at the end of your shift

Every machine you have worked on must be switched off before you leave.

5
Maintain all sheets and lists as agreed

All sheets and lists must be maintained as discussed and not changed without prior agreement.

6
Document every anomaly in the Warehouse App

Document every anomaly found on a film in the Warehouse App to inform other departments and the customer.

7
Communicate openly and in a timely manner

If you notice a problem with a machine, film or order, inform leadership immediately.

8
Never leave an active scan unattended

An active scan must always be supervised. Do not leave the machine while it is running.

9
Respect the order of jobs

Orders are processed in the agreed sequence. No exceptions without prior discussion with leadership.

10
Treat all customer data with confidentiality

Names, addresses and the contents of customer films remain internal and must not be shared outside.
